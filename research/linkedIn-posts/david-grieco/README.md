That quote had the biggest impact on how I think about GTM.

The goal of sales and marketing is to deliver the best possible experience to convert prospects into customers. Full stop.

The way I think about it: I have two sets of customers.

1. External (the prospect)
Being sold to is annoying by default. Your job is to make it not that by targeting:
→ The right people (nail your ICP) 
→ At the right time (signals) 
→ With the right message (content)

2. Internal (your sales team)
In any sales-led org, your campaign only succeeds if sales is bought in. When they're hyped, they promote, they follow-up, they compound the effect. If they don’t care, your idea will fail no matter how good it is.

I tell my team: internal marketing drives more than 50% of the success of a sales-led growth campaign.

With this mindset, I spend most of our time thinking about what a good campaign looks like: the ICP, the distribution, the content, the internal marketing strategy. I choose that. Not AI.

The content has to bring free value. That's why my team NEVER talks about features or product launches. Nobody cares.

Once I have a hypothesis I believe in, then I work backwards to the technology I need to make it happen.

We use Clay for a lot of that. But the tool really doesn't matter.
